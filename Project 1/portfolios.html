Portfolios!

Portfolios are crucial for anyone who wishes to learn more because they allow a user to keep track of what they've learned. In addition, portfolios show the user's accomplishments via blogs, multimedia, hyperlinks, etc. Portfolio's are essentially interactive learning tools that are designed in a way that it allows other users to provide feedback and comments to the original poster's learning experience.

In one's careers, portfolios are a wonderful way to record all of one's findings. For example, an IT system support specialist would record all of his/her findings in the portfolio. This would include all of the problems encountered, all the solutions, work arounds, thought processes, etc. As a result, the portfolio would demonstrate a journey through one's experiences and overall learned lessons. From there, other users can comment and add their input which could include possible solutions or feedback.

In my particular example, my preferred career is in databases and reporting which is located in IT. Similarly to the system support specialist, I would use a portfolio to record all of my problems, and solutions, along with anything that I may find useful to the specific task at hand. I may record in my portfolio something such as "Today, I have been asked by my coworker what the best way is to find all customers located in the Florida. I was able to explain that the best way to do that was to query the X database on the X server." this poses a problem and a solution to the reader. This could help anyone who has had a similar problem as the coworker in the example. Portfolios can work wonders for your career, as they show that you consistently learning and trying new things. Employers who view a portfolio can easily see the hard work and accomplishments via the portfolio option.

Summary

  1. Portfolios should be used to enchance your learning experience
  2. Portfolios can show a user's accomplishments
  3. Portfolios demonstrate to future employers that you are a worthy candidate
